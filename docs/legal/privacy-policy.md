# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 7, 2026
**Last Updated:** January 7, 2026

## Our Commitment to Your Privacy

The Integrated Health Empowerment Program ("IHEP," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and securing your Protected Health Information (PHI). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect your personal information and health data.

**HIPAA Compliance:** IHEP is designed to comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act, and applicable state privacy laws. This Privacy Policy supplements our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices.

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Personal Identifying Information (PII)
Information that identifies you as an individual:

**Account Information:**
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Date of birth
- Mailing address
- Username and password (encrypted)
- Profile photo (optional)

**Identity Verification:**
- Government-issued ID information (for account security)
- Social Security Number (last 4 digits only, for benefits matching)
- Emergency contact information

**Demographic Information:**
- Gender identity
- Race and ethnicity (optional, for health equity research)
- Preferred language
- Socioeconomic indicators (for benefits eligibility)

### 1.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)
Health-related information protected under HIPAA:

**Clinical Twin Data:**
- Medical diagnoses and conditions
- Medications and prescriptions
- Laboratory and test results
- Vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, weight, BMI)
- Immunization records
- Allergies and adverse reactions
- Treatment plans and clinical notes
- Healthcare provider information
- Insurance information

**Behavioral Twin Data:**
- Mental health assessments and diagnoses
- Substance use history and treatment
- Behavioral health appointments and progress notes
- Therapy session notes (if entered)
- Self-reported mood and mental health tracking
- Crisis intervention records

**Social Twin Data:**
- Social determinants of health (housing stability, food security, transportation access)
- Support network information
- Community resource utilization
- Social engagement activities
- Peer support group participation

**Financial Twin Data:**
- Financial health assessments
- Income information
- Benefits enrollment status
- Credit score data (if authorized)
- Financial assistance received
- Employment status and history
- Banking information (for direct payments, encrypted and tokenized)

**Wellness and Lifestyle Data:**
- Physical activity and exercise logs
- Nutrition and diet information
- Sleep patterns
- Stress levels
- Quality of life assessments
- Patient-reported outcomes

### 1.3 Usage and Technical Data
Information about how you use IHEP:

**Device Information:**
- IP address (masked for privacy)
- Device type and model
- Operating system and version
- Browser type and version
- Screen resolution
- Device identifiers (anonymized)

**Usage Analytics:**
- Pages visited and features used
- Time spent on platform
- Click patterns and navigation paths
- Search queries
- Feature engagement metrics
- Login frequency and patterns

**Communication Data:**
- Messages sent through secure messaging (encrypted)
- Forum posts and community interactions
- Feedback and support requests
- Telehealth session metadata (date, duration, provider)

### 1.4 Cookies and Tracking Technologies
We use cookies and similar technologies to:
- Maintain your logged-in session
- Remember your preferences
- Analyze platform usage and performance
- Enhance security and detect fraud

**Cookie Types:**
- **Essential Cookies:** Required for platform functionality (cannot be disabled)
- **Analytics Cookies:** Help us understand usage patterns (can be disabled)
- **Preference Cookies:** Remember your settings (can be disabled)

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may impair platform functionality.

### 1.5 Information from Third Parties
We may receive information from:

**Healthcare Providers:** Medical records, lab results, prescriptions, clinical notes (with your authorization)

**Electronic Health Records (EHRs):** Via FHIR API integrations (with your consent)

**Pharmacy Systems:** Prescription history, medication adherence data

**Health Information Exchanges (HIEs):** Consolidated health records from multiple providers

**Payment Processors:** Transaction confirmations and payment status

**Social Service Agencies:** Benefits enrollment status, resource utilization

**Public Records:** Benefits eligibility databases, community resource directories

## 3. How We Share Your Information

### 3.1 With Your Consent
We share information only with your explicit authorization:

**Healthcare Providers:** Doctors, nurses, therapists, care coordinators authorized to view your records

**Care Team Members:** Family members, caregivers, or support persons you designate

**Third-Party Apps:** Health apps and devices you choose to connect (e.g., fitness trackers, glucose monitors)

**Research Studies:** De-identified data for approved research projects (with separate research consent)

### 3.2 Healthcare Operations (HIPAA-Permitted)
We may share PHI without consent for:

**Treatment:** Coordinating care with your healthcare team, sharing records with specialists, facilitating referrals

**Payment:** Processing insurance claims, verifying coverage, billing for services

**Healthcare Operations:** Quality improvement, care coordination, case management, utilization review

**Business Associates:** Service providers bound by Business Associate Agreements (BAAs):
- Cloud hosting providers (Google Cloud Platform with BAA)
- Data analytics platforms (with encryption and access controls)
- Telehealth platform providers
- Payment processors (PCI-DSS compliant)
- Security and monitoring services

### 3.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose information when required by law:
- Court orders, subpoenas, and legal process
- Law enforcement requests (with proper legal authority)
- Public health reporting (communicable diseases, adverse events)
- Regulatory agency audits and investigations
- Workers' compensation proceedings
- Organ donation organizations (if you are a donor)

### 3.4 Public Health and Safety
We may disclose PHI without consent to:
- Prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to health or safety
- Report abuse, neglect, or domestic violence to authorities
- Assist law enforcement in locating fugitives or suspects
- Comply with military and national security requirements
- Report adverse drug events to the FDA

### 3.5 De-identified and Aggregated Data
We may share de-identified, aggregated data with:
- Research institutions for public health studies
- Policymakers for healthcare policy development
- Payers and providers for population health analytics
- Platform partners for service improvement

De-identified data cannot be linked back to you and is not subject to HIPAA restrictions.

### 3.6 Corporate Transactions
If IHEP is involved in a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will notify you and provide choices regarding your data.

## 4. Data Security and Protection

### 4.1 Technical Safeguards
We implement industry-standard security measures:

**Encryption:**
- **In Transit:** TLS 1.3 encryption for all data transmission
- **At Rest:** AES-256 encryption for stored data
- **Key Management:** Google Cloud Key Management Service (KMS) with Hardware Security Modules (HSMs)

**Access Controls:**
- Role-based access control (RBAC) with least privilege principle
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for staff accounts
- Session timeouts (30 minutes of inactivity)
- Unique user accounts (no shared credentials)

**Network Security:**
- Web Application Firewall (WAF) with Google Cloud Armor
- Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) protection
- Virtual Private Cloud (VPC) with network segmentation
- Intrusion Detection and Prevention Systems (IDS/IPS)

**Application Security:**
- Input validation and sanitization (Zod schema validation)
- Protection against SQL injection, XSS, CSRF
- Secure coding practices and code reviews
- Regular security scanning and penetration testing

**Infrastructure Security:**
- Google Cloud Platform (GCP) infrastructure with SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, HIPAA compliance
- Automated security patching and updates
- Redundant backups with encryption
- Disaster recovery and business continuity plans

### 4.2 Organizational Safeguards
We maintain comprehensive security policies and procedures:

**Staff Training:**
- Annual HIPAA and security awareness training
- Role-specific training for staff accessing PHI
- Incident response drills and tabletop exercises

**Access Management:**
- Background checks for employees accessing PHI
- Termination procedures to revoke access immediately
- Audit logs of all PHI access (who, when, what, why)
- Regular access reviews and recertification

**Vendor Management:**
- Due diligence assessments for all vendors handling PHI
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with security requirements
- Annual vendor security audits and attestations

**Incident Response:**
- 24/7 security monitoring and alerting
- Incident response team and procedures
- Breach notification process (within 60 days as required by HIPAA)
- Post-incident analysis and remediation

### 4.3 Physical Safeguards
Our data centers (Google Cloud Platform) implement:
- Restricted physical access with biometric controls
- 24/7 surveillance and security personnel
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control)
- Redundant power and network connectivity

### 4.4 Data Retention and Deletion
**Retention Periods:**
- **Active Accounts:** Data retained while account is active
- **Inactive Accounts:** Data retained for 7 years after last activity (HIPAA requirement)
- **Closed Accounts:** PHI retained for 7 years, then securely deleted
- **Backup Data:** Retained for 90 days, then permanently deleted
- **Audit Logs:** Retained for 7 years for compliance

**Secure Deletion:**
- Multi-pass overwriting to prevent recovery
- Cryptographic erasure of encryption keys
- Certification of destruction for physical media
- Verification and logging of deletion activities

### 4.5 Your Security Responsibilities
You play a critical role in protecting your data:
- Use strong, unique passwords (minimum 12 characters, mix of types)
- Enable two-factor authentication (strongly recommended)
- Never share your password with anyone
- Log out after using shared or public devices
- Report suspicious activity immediately to security@ihep.app
- Keep your email account secure (used for password resets)

## 5. Your Privacy Rights

### 5.1 HIPAA Rights
You have the following rights under HIPAA:

**Right to Access:** Request a copy of your PHI in electronic or paper format (within 30 days, free of charge for first copy)

**Right to Amend:** Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete PHI (we may deny if created by another entity or if accurate)

**Right to Accounting of Disclosures:** Receive a list of certain disclosures made in the past 6 years (excludes treatment, payment, operations, and disclosures you authorized)

**Right to Restrict:** Request limits on uses and disclosures of your PHI (we are not required to agree except for disclosures to health plans for services you paid for out-of-pocket)

**Right to Confidential Communications:** Request communications by alternative means or at alternative locations

**Right to Notice:** Receive this Privacy Policy and our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices

**Right to File a Complaint:** Complain to us at privacy@ihep.app or to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) Office for Civil Rights (OCR) without retaliation

### 5.2 State Privacy Rights
Depending on your location, you may have additional rights:

**California (CCPA/CPRA):**
- Right to know what personal information is collected, used, shared
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of sale of personal information (we do not sell data)
- Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

**Other States:** Similar rights may apply under Virginia CDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, and other emerging state privacy laws.

### 5.3 Data Portability
You can request an export of your data in machine-readable format (JSON, CSV, or FHIR):
- Personal information and account settings
- Health data from all four digital twins
- Wellness tracking and goal history
- Messages and communication logs
- Financial assessment data

We will provide the export within 30 days at no charge.

### 5.4 Account Deletion
You can delete your account at any time through account settings or by contacting privacy@ihep.app.

**Upon deletion:**
- Your account is immediately deactivated
- PHI is retained for 7 years to comply with legal requirements, then securely deleted
- De-identified data used in research remains available (cannot be linked back to you)
- We may retain limited data (email hash) to honor opt-out preferences and prevent re-registration fraud

**Cannot be reversed:** Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.

### 5.5 Marketing Opt-Out
You can opt-out of marketing communications at any time:
- Click "Unsubscribe" in marketing emails
- Adjust preferences in account settings
- Email optout@ihep.app

You will continue to receive transactional emails (account notifications, security alerts, appointment reminders) necessary for the service.

## 6. Children's Privacy

IHEP is not intended for children under 18 without parental consent. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 18 except:
- With verifiable parental consent
- When a parent or legal guardian creates and manages the account
- For emancipated minors with legal documentation

If we learn we have collected information from a child under 18 without proper authorization, we will delete it immediately. Parents can contact privacy@ihep.app to review, update, or delete their child's information.

## 7. International Data Transfers

IHEP is based in the United States, and your data is stored on servers located in the United States. If you access IHEP from outside the U.S., your information will be transferred to, processed, and stored in the U.S., which may have different data protection laws than your country.

By using IHEP, you consent to the transfer of your information to the U.S. We implement appropriate safeguards (encryption, access controls, contractual protections) to protect your data regardless of location.
